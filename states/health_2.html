What Kind Of Medicare Changes Can You Expect This Year? Find Out!

For the 2021 Medicare enrollees, there are many changes that have been made. Some of these changes apply to Medicare Advantage and Medicare Part D. These are the plans which beneficiaries can change during the annual fall enrollment period, October 15 to December 7.

However, some changes have been made to the Original Medicare high-income brackets, the cost-sharing and premiums, and more. For Medicare Part B, the standard premium in 2021 is $148.50 per month. Over the standard premium of 2020, this is an increase of less than $4 every month. It had been expected to increase more significantly, but a fixed limit was set for its increase based on a short-term spending bill that was enacted by the federal government.

The increase in the premium amounts of Part B also depends on the size of the annual Social Security cost of living adjustment (COLA). While in some years, small COLAs have restricted the amount of premium increases, this year, the COLA is 1.3%. This allows for the full Part B increase for almost all the beneficiaries of Medicare.

For this year, the Part B deductible is $203, which is on the higher side. In 2020, it was $198 and in 2019, it was $185. However, some enrollees have supplemental coverage that pays their deductibles. This includes employer-sponsored plans, Medicaid, and Medigap plans C and F. But, as of 2020, Medigap C and F plans cannot be sold to new enrollees.

Some of The Other Changes in Medicare Plans for 2021 Are as Follows:

  • Part A deductible, coinsurance, and premiums are higher
  • The income brackets for high-income premium adjustments for Medicare Parts B and D will start at $88,000 for a single individual
  • The high-income surcharges for Part D and Part B will increase in 2021.
