How to Apply for a Home Loan

Buying your first home is a huge milestone. Once you’ve found one meets your needs and budget, and the offer you made to the seller has been accepted, it’s time to apply for your loan.

First, you’ll need to select a lender. You may be able to apply for your loan in person, by phone or online. During the application process you’ll be required to provide information about yourself and your co-borrower.

Here’s a general overview of of the process:

What You’ll Need

Documentation to verify your creditworthiness, employment history and overall financial situation:

– Wage and Tax Statement/ Form W-2 for the last 2 years

– Recent pay stubs (30 days)

– Complete bank statements for the last 2 months (all financial accounts, including investments)

– Signed personal and business tax returns

– A copy of the most recent quarterly or year-to-date profit/loss statement (if self-employed)

– A copy of the signed Purchase and Sales Agreement

Depending on your circumstances and the type of mortgage you’re applying for, your lender may require additional documents and information about your employment and financial history. They’ll also require your permission to check your credit report.

Take your time and fill out the application as carefully and accurately as possible, leaving out nothing. Not being upfront about credit problems or not submitting requested documents will not only delay the process, but may also prevent mortgage approval. To improve your chances of success, be sure to fully disclose everything about your finances.

Locking in Your Interest Rate

A rate lock is a written assurance given to you by the lender guaranteeing the interest rate and discount points until the rate lock expiration date.

Locking in your rate with your lender at the time of application will protect you against rising interest rates. If, however, you believe that interest rates will fall in the near future, you may want to wait to lock in your rate.
